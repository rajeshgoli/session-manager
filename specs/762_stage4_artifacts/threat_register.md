## Stage 5 Disposition Summary

Stage 4 originally surfaced breaking-change candidates. Stage 5 now decides them for the first Rust release: accept tighter local bypass, public-edge proof, retained worker-proofed inbound email, non-loopback hook proof, explicit node credentials, narrow queue policy gates, app artifact auth/proof/signing, public browser/watch narrowing, stricter session-control authority, GitHub/Codex review allowlists or approvals, non-destructive service cutover, local/operator-only summary behavior, and public sensitive-read removal. Reject cursor/event reset, reducer semantic changes, remote `/hooks/tmux-client`, and Telegram bot/control/topic cleanup. Email/human recipient delivery and inbound email remain retained fallback surfaces, not retirement surfaces.
